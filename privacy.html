Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 25, 2026

Pulse is a personal, single-user health dashboard that displays your own Google Health / Fitbit data back to you. This policy explains what it accesses and how that information is handled.

Information Pulse accesses

With your explicit consent, Pulse requests read-only access to your own Google Health data through the Google Health API:

  • Activity & fitness — steps, distance, active minutes, calories, VO₂ max.
  • Health metrics — heart rate, heart-rate variability, SpO₂, respiratory rate, weight.
  • Sleep — stages, duration and efficiency.
  • Profile — basic account profile information.

Pulse never requests write access and cannot modify your Google data.

How your information is used

Your data is used solely to render your own dashboard — readiness, sleep, heart, steps, calories and workouts. It is processed on your own device. Pulse does not use your health data for advertising, profiling, or any purpose other than showing it to you.

Storage and sharing

Your health data is stored locally on the device or machine where you run Pulse. It is not transmitted to any server operated by the developer, and it is never sold, rented, or shared with third parties. Authentication tokens are stored locally on your machine and can be revoked at any time.

Google API Services — Limited Use

Pulse's use of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements. Google user data is used only to provide and improve the user-facing features of the app, is not transferred to others except as required to operate the app or with your consent, and is not used for advertising.

Your choices

You can revoke Pulse's access at any time from your Google Account permissions page (myaccount.google.com/permissions). Removing the local data files deletes the cached copy from your device.
